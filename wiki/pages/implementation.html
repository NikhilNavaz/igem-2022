Overview


During the duration of our project, we interacted, collaborated and exchanged ideas with multitude of teams around the globe. Ranging from synbio music video to a 3 day forum comprising talks, panel discussion, interview and quiz! Collaborations has been a golden oppurtunity for us to interact with teams who come from diverse backgrounds and nationality, appreciate the beauty of how inclusive research can be, learn from each other and grow during the iGEM duration. Our amazing set of collaborators has played a crucial role in molding and improvising our thought process, project ideation and execution. We learned the importance of collaboration and interpersonal skills. The happy ending of each collaboration definitely bought smile to our faces. Apart from spreading awareness on project or synbio, we had created a lot of joyful moments to cherish.


Group Collaborations


Brainstorming sessions with individual teams, in time collated to collaborating with multiple teams at once and organising events at much bigger scale. This enabled us to address a wider set of audience.


Collaborations We Hosted


Among the group collaborations, for some of them we came up with the basic frame of event and then invited other teams to be a part of the venture. Though we hosted such collaborations, collaborators with their suggestions made the organisation and planning for event smoother and efficient.


We-Talk

Why not gather all of the teams addressing on women's issues under one roof? This thought prompted us to go for a grand collaboration! We had recurring meetups with teams iGEM IISER Tirupati, IISER Berhampur, IIT Roorkee, Yale and Tu Delft. We brainstormed on the best way to raise awareness about these women's issues and decided to have a three-day session comprising interviews, panel discussions, and talks with people and organisation that have made pioneering efforts to enhance women's lives. Due to the hectic schedule and time constraints, we decided to go ahead with IISER Tirupati, IIT Roorkee and IISER Berhampur.

  • IISER Tirupati: Developing aptamer based diagnostic kit for PCOS
  • IISER Berhampur: The goal of their project is to develop an aptamer-based diagnostic kit to detect uropathogenic bacteria; both gram-negative (E.coli K-12) and gram positive that cause maximum cases of UTIs
  • IIT Roorkee: Developing a CRISPR/ Cas12a system to diagnose cervical cancer

We brainstormed on the best way to raise awareness about these women's issues and decided to have a three-day forum comprising interviews, panel discussions, and talks with people and organisations that have made pioneering efforts to enhance women's lives.

Figure 1. Brainstorming session


Collaborators helped us with spreading out the word, coming up with ideas finding speakers, drafting mail and designing the promotional materials.

Figure 2. Posters for Talk series


On the first day, we had a quiz session when Science meets fun, hosted by Tirapti. On the next day a talk was organised by Dr. Indira Palo, Gynecologist & Obstetrician Doctor on the topic Menstrual Hygiene and Challenges in Adolescent Girls, which was hosted by IISER Berhampur. As a continuation to that we had a session with Dr Prerna Lakhwani, Senior Gynecologic Oncologist on the topic Cervical Cancer & Breast Cancer hosted by IIT Roorkee. We wrapped up the session with a panel discussion A round-robin among collaborators and an interview with Dr. Manjiri Bakre, CEO and Founder of OncoStem Diagnostics hosted by IISER TVM.


Come work with us!

What if you get a chance to roam around labs around the globe, beyond the continents and oceans. We felt that as research students it is our responsibility to introduce the laboratory facilities, not just limited to India but that of the global research community to an inaccessible community. We hope to give wings to the kids who dream to fly high, but are limited by the barriers of land and language. As a first step towards that big dream we collaborated with iGEM Patras, IISER Tirupati, Tec CEM, ABOA Finland and IISER Mohali to bring forth a mega lab tour video with snippets of lab facilities around the globe.

iGEM University of Barcelona


The epic collaboration that evolved into an incredible partnership! It all started when iGEM UB replied to a collaboration request in slack. To our astonishment, we got to know that both teams are addressing the same issue using different components. With excitement at its peak to finally meet a team with a similar idea, we had our first meetup with UB on . They were working on addressing Burkitt Lymphoma using external vesicles (EVs) as drug delivery vesicles whereas we were working on targeting breast cancer using bacterial outer membrane vesicles (OMVs) as drug delivery vehicles. We had a couple of brainstorming sessions where we looked into the possibilities of an HP collaboration, but later decided to go ahead with discussing the overlap aspects in the proposed implementation. Continuous meetings and discussions led to a partnership at a later stage.
